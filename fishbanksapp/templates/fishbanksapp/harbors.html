Harbors

The harbors are the beating heart of the fishing economy. Each harbor acts as a home base for fishing fleets, offering storage for your catches and a location for specific fish species to spawn and thrive. Harbors vary in size, cost, and ecological richness—from small artisanal wharves to massive industrial ports.

Strategy Tips:

  • Start Small, Think Big: Lower-fee harbors like Sandbar Point are great for getting started but may limit your long-term capacity.
  • Match Your Fleet: Larger ships or high-volume fishing strategies pair well with high-capacity harbors like Ironhook Wharf or Caldera Bay.
  • Diversify: Consider spreading your operations across multiple harbors to access a wider variety of fish species and trade opportunities.
  • Storage Fee Awareness: Don’t overlook the storage fees—high-value fish can offset these, but volume without value can drain your profits.

Choose your harbor wisely—it can shape your entire fishing strategy.
